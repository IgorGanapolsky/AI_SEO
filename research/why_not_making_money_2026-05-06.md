## Root Causes

### 1. The offer is too broad for a cold buyer

"AI Workflow Revenue Diagnostic" is accurate, but it asks a cold reader to diagnose their own need. The market does not buy generic AI help quickly. Same-day buyers need a painful, named problem: missed restaurant calls, broken n8n lead routing, failed agent handoffs, or affiliate tracking leaks.

Fix: create vertical offer pages with problem-specific headlines and deliverables.

### 2. There is no warm trust bridge yet

Cold social traffic to a $499 checkout has low conversion unless the buyer already trusts the operator or sees an urgent pain. Current public proof is a newly launched site and one social push.

Fix: route distribution through specific posts, proof files, and niche communities where buyers already have the problem.

### 3. SEO is not a same-day money channel

Programmatic SEO is compounding infrastructure. It can produce affiliate revenue later, but it does not create buyer intent the day the pages are published.

Fix: keep SEO pages, but sell a service offer today.

### 4. Affiliate links are not approved tracking links yet

Several pages link to affiliate application pages or public partner pages, not issued tracking links. Even if a buyer clicks today, attribution may not produce revenue.

Fix: treat affiliate revenue as future upside until partner dashboards issue tracking URLs.

### 5. The funnel has no intake capture before checkout

The current page asks for purchase before collecting a lighter signal. Some buyers who might be interested will not checkout immediately.

Fix: add a low-friction "send details" path and publish posts that ask for a specific reply, not only a purchase.

### 6. Distribution volume is too low

One cross-post is not enough. 2026 outbound benchmarks suggest low single-digit reply rates for cold email and similarly noisy LinkedIn environments unless targeting is narrow and signal-based.

Fix: publish multiple vertical posts and send targeted outreach only to leads with a specific reason to care.
